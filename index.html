About Us

The Women Cell is dedicated to fostering a safe, inclusive, and empowering environment for all women within the institution. We strive to create a campus culture where women feel respected, supported, and heard. Through awareness programs, interactive sessions, workshops, and counseling services, we aim to educate the community about gender sensitivity, rights, and the importance of mutual respect.

Our initiatives are designed not only to address immediate concerns but also to inspire long-term change in attitudes and behaviors. By collaborating with students, faculty, and staff, the Cell works to promote gender equality and ensure that everyone has access to the resources and support they need.

Safety Guidelines & Tips

We prioritize safety and well-being. Please follow these key guidelines to ensure a safe and supportive environment:

General Safety Tips

  • Trust your instincts. If something feels off, it probably is.
  • Always share your location or plans with a trusted friend or family member.
  • Keep emergency contacts on speed dial. Learn basic self-defense techniques.
  • Stay alert and avoid distractions (like using your phone or wearing headphones) in unfamiliar areas.

Home Safety

  • Always lock your doors and windows, even when you're at home.
  • Do not open the door to strangers. Use a peephole or security camera if available.
  • Avoid revealing on social media that you’re home alone or going out.
  • Install security systems or motion-sensor lights if possible.

Travel & Transportation

  • Avoid isolated stops, especially at night.
  • Use trusted ride services and share ride details with someone.
  • Sit in the back seat and check the driver’s ID before getting in.
  • Keep your phone accessible during rides.

Online Safety

  • Never share personal info like address or phone number online.
  • Use strong, unique passwords for your accounts.
  • Be cautious of strangers contacting you through social media.
  • Meet online acquaintances in public, never alone.

Public Safety

  • Walk in well-lit, busy areas and avoid isolated paths.
  • Carry a whistle or pepper spray if allowed.
  • If followed, go into a public place and call someone.
  • Walk confidently and stay aware of your surroundings.

Emergency Tools & Apps

  • Use apps like 112 India, bSafe, or My Safetipin.
  • Set up emergency contacts in your phone.
  • Always keep your phone charged when outside.
  • Keep a small power bank with you for emergencies.
